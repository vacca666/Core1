CHOCOLATE BIRTHDAY CAKE!

This weekend was my birthday. I decided to make my own cake, since I wanted to challenge myself and was missing my sister, so I dedicated this to her. My younger sister is away at school and she loves baking, so I have been craving some homemade baked goods and was looking to share my process with her while we are apart. I love chocolate and my dream cake is a brooklyn blackout cake or the chooclate ganache cake featured in one of our favorite shows, The Great British Bake Off.

INGREDIENT LIST!
First, I made my sponge. I used a Brooklyn Blackout cake recipe, with the most important ingredient being dutch processed cocoa powder (extra rich, cocoa powder). They bake for about 35 minutes, and in the meantime, I got started on the frostings. The filling was meant to be a chocolate ganache, although I ultimately failed at following a recipe for it because I used incorrect chocolate. But it was still okay, I guess. I didn't use a recipe for the frosting on the outside, but added ingredients such as both regular and dutch processed cocoa powder, powdered sugar, melted butter, vanilla, and instant espresso!
Once all elements were ready, I assembled, placing the ganache in between cake layers, and frosting the outside. The finishing touch was just to add sprinkles. Overall, I think the feat of making my own cake turned out to be delicious, except for the hiccup of my ganache.
I sent pictures to my sister, and we discussed the fact that she misses having an oven and being able to easily bake for her loved ones. Although I shared my cake through the screen with her, I'm sure she would have liked to have a piece in person.



TA-DA!
